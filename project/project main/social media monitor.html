Social Media Monitoring

Social media monitoring plays a critical role in understanding public sentiment, identifying harmful content, and ensuring digital platforms remain safe and inclusive. In this project, social media monitoring focuses on analyzing YouTube comments to detect instances of hate speech. By leveraging Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques and a trained transformer-based classification model, the system continuously evaluates user-generated content. This enables stakeholders such as researchers, moderators, or organizations to gain insights into how users interact with video content and how frequently hate-related expressions appear. The ability to track and analyze such behavior in near real-time helps in addressing digital abuse, promoting respectful dialogue, and shaping better community guidelines on social platforms.

Real-time Analysis

Our system processes social media content as it's posted, providing immediate alerts when potential hate speech is detected, allowing for timely intervention.

Single-Platfrom Coverage

Our project focuses on single-platform coverage by exclusively monitoring and analyzing hate speech within YouTube comments.

Contextual Understanding

Contextual understanding enables the model to interpret the meaning of comments based on surrounding words, improving the accuracy of hate speech detection.

Trend Analysis

Trend analysis in our project helps detect evolving patterns of hate speech over time, enabling proactive monitoring of harmful content on YouTube.
